      Helen mccourt death

Updated on Jun 29, 2022 by Admin.

A woman whose daughter was murdered hopes the killer's death means she will finally learn what happened to her daughter's remains.

Ian Simms, who died recently, was imprisoned in 1989 for Helen McCourt's murder but never revealed the location of her body and was released in 2020.

Marie McCourt, who advocated for a change in parole laws, said she hoped he had told someone in jail where she was.

She went on to say that learning of Simms' death was "the best news I've ever had."

Mrs McCourt's 22-year-old daughter went missing on her way home from work in St Helens, Merseyside, in 1988, and pub landlord Simms was sentenced to life in prison a year later after being found guilty of murder based on overwhelming DNA evidence.

He maintained his innocence throughout, and despite Mrs. McCourt's appeals, he never revealed where or how he disposed of Helen's body.

Mrs. McCourt's advocacy resulted in the Prisoners (Disclosure of Information About Victims) Act, also known as Helen's Law, becoming law in 2021.

It makes parole more difficult for killers and serious sex offenders who withhold information, though it does allow release if they are no longer deemed a risk.

However, as part of their assessment, the Parole Board is now legally required to consider whether they have cooperated with inquiries.
