      California release of concealed carry list

Updated on Jul 01, 2022 by Admin.

According to the Boston Globe, the Supreme Court issued a decision last week striking down a New York gun law that imposes unconstitutional restrictions on concealed carry in public.

It appears that the California Attorney General leaked the state database containing the names and addresses of the state's legal gun owners and concealed carry permit holders less than a week later.

Is this an unintentional or intentional occurrence? California gun owners applauded the Supreme Court's decision, which reaffirmed that state officials cannot require concealed carry applicants to demonstrate "proper cause" and "a special need for self-defense distinguishable from that of the general community."

"On Monday, the California Department of Justice's 2022 Firearms Dashboard Portal went live, with publicly accessible files containing identifying information for those with concealed carry permits." The leaked information includes the person's full name, race, home address, date of birth, and permit issuance date. The information also includes the type of permit issued, as well as whether the permit holder is a member of law enforcement or a judge."

According to The Reload, they reviewed a copy of the Lost Angeles County database and discovered 244 judge permits listed in the database, including all of their home addresses, full names, and dates of birth. "The same was true for seven detention officers, 63 people with a work permit, and 420 reserve officers."

Gov. Gavin Newsom signed Assembly Bill 171 into law last year, allowing the state to share personal information about gun owners with gun violence research organizations.

According to AB 171 bill analysis, "the DOJ may, at its discretion, also disclose specified information to other nonprofit bona fide research institutions accredited by the United States Department of Education or the Council for Higher Education Accreditation for the study of violence prevention in the matter prescribed." Any material identifying individuals must only be used for research and statistics, and any reports or publications derived from the material must not identify any specific individuals. Requires the Department of Justice to establish procedures for these requests and allows researchers to be charged reasonable fees in connection with their requests."

According to The Reload, the office of Attorney General Rob Bonta confirmed that private information had been exposed and that they are investigating the situation.

"We are investigating an exposure of individuals' personal information connected to the DOJ Firearms Dashboard," the office's spokesperson told The Reload. "Any unauthorized disclosure of personally identifiable information is unacceptable." We are addressing this situation as quickly as possible and will provide additional information as soon as possible."

According to The Reload, the California Rifle Pistol Association "slammed the leak and said it was looking into potential legal action against the state."

"Vengeful sore loser bureaucrats have endangered people's lives and invited conflict by illegally disclosing confidential private information," CRPA President Chuck Michel told The Reload. "CRPA is collaborating with a number of legislators and sheriffs to assess the extent of the harm caused by the DOJ's doxing of law-abiding gun owners." Litigation is highly likely."
