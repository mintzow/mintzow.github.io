      Bibb county shooting



A massive manhunt has been launched after two deputies were shot during a pursuit with a stolen car in Bibb County, Alabama, on Wednesday afternoon. Watch the video above to learn more.

According to District Attorney Michael Jackson, the deputies were shot near Brierfield in the Bulldog Bend area off Highway 25. One is in critical condition, according to Jackson, while the other is "doing fine." Watch Jackson's interview with WVTM 13 below or here.

On Facebook Wednesday night, the Centreville Press identified the two deputies as Chris Poole and Brad Johnson.

One deputy was airlifted to Birmingham's UAB Hospital. As the ambulance traveled north up Interstate 65, authorities from various agencies assisted in blocking traffic.

It's unclear where the other deputy is being treated at this time, but Jackson believes he's in a Birmingham hospital.

The suspect, Austin Hall, 26, is still at large. The manhunt is being assisted by a number of law enforcement agencies. According to court records, Hall has a lengthy criminal history dating back to 2012, including assault, burglary, attempting to elude, property theft, and injuring a police officer.


According to the Alabama Law Enforcement Agency, Hall is 5 feet 9 inches tall and weighs 169 pounds. His hair is brown and his eyes are hazel.

According to ALEA, Hall is "armed and extremely dangerous." Hall was last seen in the Cahaba Wildlife Management Area in Bibb County, near Highway 25 and Golfers Trail. Residents in the Brierfield area are being asked to remain inside and keep their doors locked while the search is ongoing.

When Hall escaped from a work release facility in Wilcox County in 2019, WVTM 13 discovered a photo of him from a 2019 news release from the Alabama Department of Corrections.

In Bibb County, a police staging area has been set up at the intersection of Bulldog Bend and Highway 25. Law enforcement agencies ranging from local departments to the FBI are assisting in the manhunt for Hall, according to ALEA.

A vigil for the two deputies was held in Centreville around 8 p.m. Wednesday. Dozens of people gathered in a circle around City Hall to pray for the deputies, their families, and first responders.
